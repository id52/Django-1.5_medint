As a cancer survivor, Dr. Librett is a past member of the Lance Armstrong Foundation's Public Health Advisory Board, Clinical Director of the American Medical Athletic Association's Clinical Advisory Board, and past chair of the White House Federal Interagency Health Council on Preventive Medicine. He is also a former senior policy advisor with the Centers for Disease Control, a member of the National Institutes of Health Clinical Conference Series in Preventive Medicine, and past Founder and Chair of a Cooper Institute Conference.

Dr. John Librett is internationally recognized for his leadership in preventive medicine, clinical translation and health policy. On these issues, he has worked with the White House, the US Department of Health and Human Services, the World Health Organization, the Pan American Health Organization and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, as well as local and state governments and corporations in Asia, Eastern Europe, Central and South America, and the U.S.
